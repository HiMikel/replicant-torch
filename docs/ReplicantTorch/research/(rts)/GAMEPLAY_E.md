# Replicant Torch - RTS Gameplay Design

## Core Gameplay Loop

Real-time strategy gameplay adapted for space exploration and colonization.

### 1. Resource Management

#### Primary Resources

- Nutrients - Organic materials for probe creation and synthesis
- Structurals - Building materials for stations and structures
- Metals - Rare elements for weapons and advanced systems
- Gold - Currency for trade and advanced technologies

#### Resource Gathering

Probes travel from the Torch (mother ship) to resource nodes:

- Asteroids - Rich in Structurals and Metals
- Ice Moons - Provide Nutrients
- Nebula Clouds - Harvest Metals
- Debris Fields - Mixed resources from destroyed vessels
- Enemy Structures - Can be captured or salvaged

Travel time creates strategic decisions about placement of refineries and gatherers.

#### Era Progression System

- Era 1: Dawn - Basic probes and structures
- Era 2: Expansion - Advanced probes, refineries
- Era 3: Industrial - Factories, military vessels
- Era 4: Federation - Maximum tech, capital ships

Each era unlocks new units, structures, and technologies.

### 2. Economy Building

#### The Torch

The central command vessel that:

- Produces probes (costs Nutrients)
- Acts as resource drop-off point
- Can research technologies
- Is vulnerable to attack - placement matters

#### Probe Management

- 3-6 probes in Dawn Era
- 10-15+ probes in Expansion for strong economy
- Allocation: typically 50-60% on Nutrients, rest on Structurals/Metals/Gold
- Probes can fight but are weak without upgrades

#### Economy Structures

- Refineries - Process raw materials
- Solar Arrays - Generate power
- Trade Stations - Enable resource trading
- Factories - Produce military units

### 3. Military and Combat

#### Unit Categories

| Category | Examples | Counter |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Fighters | Scout, Interceptor, Fighter | Bombers, Frigates |
| Bombers | Light Bomber, Heavy Bomber | Fighters, Frigates |
| Frigates | Assault Ship, Destroyer | Battleships, Bombers |
| Battleships | Capital Ship, Dreadnought | Frigates, Fighters |
| Probes | Collector Probe, Combat Probe | Fighters, Military |

#### Combat Mechanics

- Unit counter system - fighters beat bombers, frigates beat battleships
- Formation fighting - units fight better in groups
- Range advantages - longer range weapons have advantage
- Upgrade systems - technology upgrades improve units

#### Strategy Types

- Expansion - Focus on economy, reach Federation Era fast
- Raiding - Early attacks to disrupt opponent economy
- Defense - Stationary defense, turtle play
- Timing Attack - Military push at specific power spikes

### 4. Victory Conditions

- Conquest - Destroy all enemy Torches
- Expansion - Control majority of resource nodes
- Research - Complete all technology trees
- Survival - Last player remaining

## Strategic Depth

### Macro Strategy

- Build Order - Sequence of probe production and building
- Resource Allocation - Probes per resource type
- Era Timing - When to advance eras
- Military Composition - What vessels to produce
- Map Control - Holding resource-rich zones

### Micro Strategy

- Idle Torch Management - Always produce probes
- Probe Redistribution - Rebalance as needed
- Fleet Positioning - Flanking, defensive positioning
- Hit and Run - Moving in and out of combat
- Focus Fire - Targeting specific enemy vessels

## Key Design Elements

### Core Mechanics

1. Multiple Resource Types - Nutrients, Structurals, Metals, Gold
2. Travel Time Mechanic - Strategic placement of refineries/gatherers
3. Era Progression - Tech tree advancement unlocking new options
4. Counter System - Military units need clear counters
5. Victory Conditions - Multiple ways to win

### Unique Features for Replicant Torch

- Self-Replication - Probes print themselves from the Torch
- Space Setting - Asteroids, planets, space stations as resource nodes
- Torch Mechanic - Mother ship as central hub
- Still Animations - Focus on strategy over flashy graphics
